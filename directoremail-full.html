Dear DP, before presenting to you the whole extremely serious and totally not exaggerated structure of DuoLínguas Private School, I think it is fair to explain one thing.

When you accepted this idea, you probably imagined something like some calls, maybe a PDF, some useful sentences, a “where is the bathroom?”, and maybe, in a moment of extreme doubt, an audio from me trying to explain something.

The problem is that you know me.

So obviously this became a mega project with visual identity, welcome kit, contract, app, schedule, structure, its own universe, and probably more documents than some real projects in the company.

But I need to make one thing very clear.

Stop saying that I should not have done this, or that it should not be too much work. Because honestly? It was delicious to do all of this.

And I know this may sound strange when someone spends 2 days creating a complete fictional language institution for one specific person, and maybe we will not even use half of it, but the truth is that this made me feel absurdly good.

You were the initial trigger of the idea, of course. But at some point during the process, I realized I was doing this much more for myself than for you.

And this does not reduce the value of the delivery, because it did not come from obligation, pressure, or “I need to impress you”.

Do not think I did this to impress you. If that was the case, I would have tattooed Poutana on my ass.

This came from a place that I honestly had not visited in many years.

Before working with support, incidents, providers, and people writing “we are checking and will revert”, I was from the creative area.

Fashion. Backstage. Production. Scenography. Fashion shows. Parties.

And I think this explains a lot about me.

I never really wanted to be the center of the experience, but I always liked to build experiences for other people to live.

So, suddenly, I was here creating a fictional language school at 3am, discussing typography, making a fake contract, planning a diploma, thinking about physical material, organizing visual identity, and genuinely having fun with it.

For an ADHD brain like mine, this was better than Disney.

And probably better than a Greek nudist beach full of naked daddies walking freely.

And this is a very difficult competition.

The difference is that this time, for the first time in a long time, the idea did not die in 24 hours.

Months ago, I created two fake profiles for a secret international communication system and abandoned everything after.

Roberta almost had an official account, and so did the radioactive mutant squirrel mixed with orangutan.

But this time it was different.

Maybe because of Vyvanse. Maybe because I am in a better moment.

Maybe because, for the first time, I managed to sustain a creative idea without letting it explode and disappear immediately after.

Or maybe because, deep down, I really missed this version of myself.

So no, I do not want you to read this thinking: “my God, how much work.”

Because if you only see work, you are looking at all of this in the wrong way.

Without meaning to, you put me for a few days in a creative place that I honestly thought I had lost.

And because of that, no matter what happens with Portuguese, with the classes, with the project, or with the real possibility of you surviving linguistically in Brazil without accidentally buying dick instead of bread, I already consider this idea a success.

 
 
 

Regards,

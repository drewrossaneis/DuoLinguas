BERRINI · BROOKLIN · ITAIM BIBI · VILA OLÍMPIA · FARIA LIMA
This is where your hotel and workplace are. Corporate towers, private security everywhere, Uber on every corner, cafés with badges and overpriced coffee. During business hours, this is one of the most controlled environments in São Paulo. The problem starts when you assume the rest of the city works the same way. It does not.
DISTANCE FROM HOTEL
0 km — you are here
UBER AVAILABILITY
High — usually under 3 min
DAYTIME
Comfortable. Walk freely.
NIGHTTIME
Still relatively safe. Stay on main avenues. Phone in pocket.
WHAT TO KNOW
The corporate bubble creates a false sense of security. The city changes completely beyond Av. Santo Amaro and Marginal Pinheiros. Do not use Berrini as your reference for the rest of São Paulo.
WATCH OUT FOR
Phone theft near busy intersections. Motorcycle approach at traffic lights. Empty side streets after 10pm.
Caution
MOEMA · CAMPO BELO · SANTO AMARO · MORUMBI · PINHEIROS
Transition zone. Still upper-middle class, still relatively safe during the day. But you are leaving the corporate bubble. Fewer private security cameras, less constant pedestrian flow, more variance by street and time. Google Maps may say 10 minutes walk. Check the route first.
DISTANCE FROM HOTEL
2–6 km — short Uber ride
UBER AVAILABILITY
Good — wait may vary
TRAVEL TIME (UBER)
15–40 min depending on traffic
DAYTIME
Fine. Normal city behavior.
NIGHTTIME
Pay attention. Avoid isolated streets. Stay near busy areas.
WHAT TO KNOW
These neighborhoods look very similar to Berrini on the surface. The difference is in the details: lighting, pedestrian density, and what happens at the end of the block.
WATCH OUT FOR
Less predictable traffic. Phone visible on street. Don't walk back to the hotel alone late at night.
Avoid at night
VILA ANDRADE · CAPÃO REDONDO DIRECTION · OUTER ZONA SUL
Not where you will be staying. But São Paulo is a city where a 5km trip can completely change your context. If you venture beyond the bubble — intentionally or by Uber route — this is what exists on the other side. Not impossible, but requires awareness, planning, and ideally company.
DISTANCE FROM HOTEL
8–15 km — feels close, it is not
UBER AVAILABILITY
Lower — surge pricing common
TRAVEL TIME (UBER)
40–90 min in peak hours
DAYTIME
Manageable with attention. Don't go alone to unfamiliar streets.
NIGHTTIME
Strongly avoid unless with trusted local company.
WHAT TO KNOW
São Paulo's Zona Sul has the highest crime growth rates in the city. Capão Redondo and surroundings consistently rank among the most dangerous districts. Distance on the map is misleading.
WATCH OUT FOR
Motorcycle theft. Armed robbery near bus terminals. Long waits for Uber after 10pm with no safe spot to wait.
High risk
CENTRO HISTÓRICO · SÉ · REPÚBLICA · LUZ · BRÁS · LIBERDADE
The historic center. Landmarks, culture, architecture, and the highest robbery rates in the city. The Praça da Sé district alone registered thousands of robberies in a single year — the highest in São Paulo's historical record. Go with purpose, go during the day, go alert. Liberdade is worth visiting. So is Pinacoteca. Just don't treat it like Berrini.
DISTANCE FROM HOTEL
12–16 km from Berrini
UBER AVAILABILITY
High — central area, many cars
TRAVEL TIME (UBER)
30–75 min — São Paulo traffic
DAYTIME
Go. Stay on main streets. Phone out of sight at all times.
NIGHTTIME
Avoid unless specific planned event with safe return.
WHAT TO KNOW
Pickpockets and phone theft are extremely common near tourist spots. This is a high-volume crime area — not because it's violent, but because there are many targets and fast exits.
WORTH VISITING
Liberdade (Japanese neighborhood) · Pinacoteca · Mercadão · Theatro Municipal · MASP (Paulista, adjacent)
WATCH OUT FOR
Phone on hand near Sé metro. Strangers approaching with distraction tactics. Isolated alleyways.
